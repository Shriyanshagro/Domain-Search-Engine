Use Policy

Pew Research Center provides its research – free of charge – as a public service to policymakers, researchers, journalists and the general public, and encourages the use of our material in its original form.

Statement of Neutrality

As a strictly nonpartisan, non-advocacy organization, we do not grant permission for reprints, links, citations or other uses of our data and analysis that in any way imply that Pew Research Center endorses a cause, candidate, issue, party, product, business or religion.

Any use of our materials that includes our branding or trademark must be approved in writing ahead of time. (Please see our full terms & conditions.)

Please note: We do not grant permission for third-party translations of Pew Research Center publications or charts. If you operate a website that publishes in a language other than English, we suggest that you link directly to our material using an overview that you have written in the local language.

You DO NOT need permission to:

Cite Pew Research Center findings: You may cite our research in your work without advance, express permission. Creating a short excerpt from one of our reports, referencing a statistic and using our data to create your own chart are all permissible uses. We do require that you include attribution to Pew Research Center for these uses of our research. In addition, you do not need to obtain permission in advance to use our charts if you are:

  • A journalist reporting on a news story
  • A government entity or an employee of a government acting in your official capacity
  • A nonprofit research institution

For academic purposes, we recommend that you include the following information in your citation, which may be altered to suit your citation style:

  • “Report Title.” Pew Research Center, Washington, D.C. (Publication date) URL, date of access if accessed online.
  • Example: “Unauthorized Immigration Population: National and State Trends, 2010.” Pew Research Center, Washington, D.C. (February 1, 2011). http://www.pewhispanic.org/2011/02/01/unauthorized-immigrant-population-brnational-and-state-trends-2010/.

Use in a Classroom or Group Setting: If you are making a private presentation (such as a teacher presenting a lesson in the classroom or clergy presenting in a place of worship), you may make copies available to participants or create password-protected online links to our material, provided that you include attribution to Pew Research Center. Online classrooms may also use our materials under this policy.

Personal Use: You may make reasonable copies of select portions/excerpts of our content, solely for your own noncommercial personal use.

Link to Pew Research Center websites: We invite you to include links to Pew Research Center websites, publications or interactives and charts on your own site. If you link to our content, we would like to know – please notify us by email at Licensing@pewresearch.org and point us to where you’ve cited our research.

Questionnaires: Pew Research Center questions may be used freely without advance, express permission. If you are comparing your results to ours, cite those findings, as detailed above (please also consider possible contextual differences when doing this.) If you are replicating questions without directly comparing your results to ours, a citation is not required (but is welcome.)

You DO need our permission to:

Reproduce our work: Reproducing, distributing or selling any information published by Pew Research Center, for purposes other than those listed above, requires our express, advance written permission. Please use our form to submit your permission request. All permission requests will be addressed promptly; you must wait for a response before using our material.

Use our Datasets: Pew Research Center makes its data available to the public for secondary analysis. Typically, survey data are released several months after the reports written from the data are published. Before downloading the dataset, you will be required to read and accept our dataset agreement. All publications using data should reference the Pew Research Center as the source of the data, and should acknowledge that Pew Research Center bears no responsibility for the interpretations presented or conclusions reached based on analysis of the data.

About Pew Research Center Pew Research Center is a nonpartisan fact tank that informs the public about the issues, attitudes and trends shaping America and the world. It conducts public opinion polling, demographic research, media content analysis and other empirical social science research. Pew Research Center does not take policy positions. It is a subsidiary of The Pew Charitable Trusts.
