ISIS releases video purporting to show beheading of 21 Egyptians in Libya

WARNING: GRAPHIC CONTENT The grisly video shows militants dressed in black marching the captives to a beach, where they are all beheaded.

BY Bill Hutchinson

Savage ISIS terrorists released one of their most grisly snuff videos Sunday, showing the seaside beheadings of 21 Egyptian Christians in Libya.

The heinous footage believed to have been taken on a beach near Tripoli in recent days was posted on Twitter by Libyan jihadists who have pledged allegiance to the Islamic State, officials said.

While the authenticity of the video remained murky Sunday night, Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi called for seven days of mourning.

“These cowardly actions will not undermine our determination,” Sisi said. “Egypt and the whole world are in a fierce battle with extremist groups carrying extremist ideology and sharing the same goals.”

Sisi said in a nationally televised speech Sunday that Egypt will choose the “necessary means and timing to avenge the criminal killings.”

Victims of the slaughter, all men of Coptic Christian faith, were kidnapped in December and January in Sirte, a coastal town in eastern Libya now under ISIS control, officials said.

The killings are the first the bloodthirsty extremist group has videotaped outside territory it has seized in Syria and Iraq.

The five-minute video shows the terrified captives, dressed in orange jumpsuits, being marched in single file along a beach by execution-ers clad in black and wielding daggers.

The victims are forced to kneel in the sand before they are beheaded one by one as crashing waves are heard in the background.

“The sea you have hidden Sheik Osama Bin Laden’s body in, we swear to Allah we will mix it with your blood,” the lead executioner, wearing a brown mask, says into the camera.

The killer speaks in fluent English, mentioning places in Syria where previous beheadings have been carried out. ISIS said the killings were revenge for “Muslim women persecuted by Coptic crusaders in Egypt.”

“Oh, people, recently you have seen us on the hills of as-Sham and Dabiq’s plain, chopping off the heads that have been carrying the cross for a long time,” he says, holding up a sharp dagger.

“Today, we are on the south of Rome, on the land of Islam, Libya, sending another message,” the head executioner says, apparently referring to Italy 500 miles to the north.

A caption in the video makes clear that the victims were targeted because of their religion. “The people of the cross, followers of the hostile Egyptian church,” the caption reads.

“Safety for you crusaders is something you can only wish for,” the lead executioner says.

The gruesome footage includes the logo for Al Hayat, the ISIS publishing arm.

Since the 2011 uprising in their country, Egyptians by the thousands have ignored danger warnings from their government and have migrated to Libya in search of work.

The video comes just a day after ISIS seized control of Sirte, the birthplace of the late Libyan dictator Moammar Khadafy.

Relatives said most of the murdered victims were from poor villages in Egypt.

“What can we say. Can someone tell us if this is true?” Qalini Sanyout, whose two nephews were among the beheaded hostages, said to The Associated Press. “The whole village is mourning. Men are covering their heads with dust and mud.”

A tearful Ibtassal Lami said she recognized her son, Samuel Walham, as one of the hostages in the video.

“Look at my love, look how beautiful he is,” Lami told the AP. “He only went there to earn hisliving.”
