Kid from Flight of the Navigator has robbed a bank

Looks like Joey Cramer is in trouble with the law. Again. The child actor, now 42, was arrested on May 1st after the Royal Canadian Mounted Police linked him to a small town bank robbery in British Colombia.

It happened in Sechelt, a coastal town of just 8,000 people. According to reports, the suspect demanded the money and then fled on foot. Oh, and he was also rockin a pretty lame disguise. Cramer was seen on surveillance footage wearing sunglasses, a bandanna, and a shoulder-length wig. Most people probably just confused him for an American on his way to a Bon Jovi concert.

But that didn’t fool the good old RCMP. They know the difference between Clark Kent and Superman, or in this case, a down and out former child hood star and the local con artist. See, this wasn’t Joey Cramer’s first run-in with the law.

Joe Cramer mug shot from previous arrest

Joe Cramer was a wanted man back in 2011. Under his many given birth names, Deleriyes August Joe Fisher Cramer, he wrote a series of fake checks and had seven warrants out for that and other fraud-related crimes. I mean, he bullshitted his was through outer space, no wonder he thought he could con his way into some fast cash.

One could argue this is a product of Hollywood, but living out in Hollywood you meet more than your fair share of normal, non-criminally minded and not-drug addled former child actors. These are just the ones that end up in the news.

After 1987, there wasn’t much left of Hollywood for little Joey C. To save you the IMDB search, his first role was when he was just 9-years-old as Bobby Ramsay in the Michael Crichton/Tom Selleck sci-fi thriller Runaway. He had two minor parts until landing his big role as David Freeman, the child taken out of his time in Flight of the Navigator. Of course, it’s only an eight year time skip, but for a 12-year-old kid, that’s a lot to have missed!

Cramer only had two credited roles after that, and didn’t appear in film again until ten years later as an uncredited extra in the progressive (for it’s time) film It’s My Party. And that’s according to his IMDB page, and whoever maintains that.

After what we can only assume was returning to life as a normal kid, Joey apparently took a job at a sporting goods store in British Colombia, and the rest is in the records of the legal system.

Cramer’s first appearance in court for the bank robbery was today, May 4th, 2016.
