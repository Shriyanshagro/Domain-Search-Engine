Press publication on ISCC and sustainability certification

How Beverage Cartons Become more Sustainable with Sabic's Renewable Polyethylene

Press release, PROCESS worldwide, 5 February 2016

More and more consumers know about their CO2-Footprint and are increasingly demanding climate-neutral packages. The packaging enterprise Elopak wants to achieve absolute sustainability by 2020. In 2014, Elopak became the first company in the liquid packaging industry to introduce a carton made of 100% renewable materials. In the need to find a truly sustainable coating, the company decided to work with the chemical company Sabic, which offers a portfolio of both renewable polyethylene (PE) and polypropylenes (PP), certified under the ISCC PLUS certification scheme, an independent system for the chemical industry to prove sustainability of bio-based products.

Mass balance to help overcome sustainability issues in bulk chemicals

F.O.Lichts Renewable Chemicals, Vol. 3 No. 23, August 6, 2014

The article highlights the advantages of the mass balance approach to increase the use of biomass in the chemical industry. Furthermore, an overview is given on global, European and national initiatives to promote increasing shares of sustainably produced biomass in this market.

ISCC PLUS is presented as multistakeholder and multi-feedstock scheme that can be used for the chemical industry to demonstrate the sustainability of the bio-based feedstocks and traceability through the supply chain. ISCC PLUS allows the application of mass balances. Furthermore, due to the acceptance of ISCC for biofuels under ISCC PLUS chemical companies could potentially access a huge supply base of ISCC certified producer of feedstock.

Impacts of sustainability certification on bioenergy markets and trade

Strategic Inter-Task Study: Monitoring Sustainability Certification of Bioenergy

February 2013

The report is part of a wider study commissioned by IEA Bioenergy, inter alia, aims to monitor the implementation process of sustainability certification in the bioenergy sector, to evaluate how stakeholders are affected by certification schemes, and to qualify the impact of worldwide bioenergy trade. Through a worldwide survey and interviews with certified companies substantial feedback of the industry involved was gained.

The report recognises ISCC as the most popular and widely used certification scheme. As reasons are stated that ISCC can be "operated globally across multiple feedstocks", has the "right balance between pragmatic operation and robust verification", combines "excellent sustainability with excellent chain of custody guarantees" offers "simple processes and tools" and is "cost competitive".

IEA Bioenergy was set up by the International Energy Agency (IEA) to improve the cooperation and the information exchange between countries with programmes in bioenergy research, development and deployment.
