I Hate Fairyland 1

Madly Ever After

by Skottie Young

School And Library,

Green-curled Gert only looks like a little girl in fact, she's been trapped in Fairyland for 27 years, and she's had it with the candy-coated wonderland. Her solution? Kill everything.
  • No Pink, But Plenty Of Red, In Hack-N-Slash 'Fairyland'

Sleeping Giants

by Sylvain Neuvel

Hardcover, 304 pages

Years after waking up dozens of feet below the ground on the palm of what seems to be a mysterious giant metal hand, a top-level physicist leads a team of people to discover the nature of the hand, where it came from, and what it portends for humanity.
  • 'Sleeping Giants' Kicks Off A New Series In Style

Join

by Steve Toutonghi

Hardcover, 328 pages

In a world where you can lead multiple lives simultaneously, have constant, perfect companionship and never die, two best friendsChance and Leapdiscover that this miracle is hiding unspeakable horrors from the world and embark on a quest for answers and survival after they are targeted by a remorseless killer.
  • Humanity And Technology Merge In Uneven But Intriguing 'Join'

The Everything Box

by Richard Kadrey

Hardcover, 352 pages

An angel, poised to destroy the last remnants of humanity in 22000 BC, discovers that he's lost the device set to end it all until it turns up in the hands of a thief of magical objects in 2015.
  • Angels, Mobsters, Monsters: This 'Box' Really Does Have Everything

The Regional Office Is Under Attack!

by Manuel Gonzales

Hardcover, 416 pages

When a prophecy suggests that an insider might bring about the downfall of a powerful underground organization, devoted recruit Sarah and young assassin Rose find their respective lives clashing in a dispute that threatens everything they know.
  • The 'Regional Office' Doesn't Quite Deliver

The Story of Kullervo

by J.R.R. Tolkien and Verlyn Flieger

Hardcover, 168 pages

The first official publication of a previously unknown work follows the story of a hapless orphan who swears revenge on the dark magician responsible for the death of his father, in a volume complemented by author drafts, notes, and lecture essays.
  • Frodo, Bilbo, Kullervo: Tolkien's Finnish Adventure

Every Heart a Doorway

by Seanan McGuire

Hardcover, 173 pages

Sent away to a home for children who have tumbled into fantastical other worlds and are looking for ways to return, Nancy triggers dark changes among her fellow schoolmates and resolves to expose the truth when a child dies under suspicious magical circumstances.

  • 'Every Heart' Is A Doorway To Winning Fantasy

Patience

by Daniel Clowes

Hardcover, 177 pages

A psychedelic science-fiction love story from the author of Ghost World veers from violent destruction to deeply personal tenderness.
  • True Love And Time Travel In 'Patience'

The Winged Histories

by Sofia Samatar

Hardcover, 335 pages

Four women, caught up on opposing sides of a violent rebellion, struggle to make sure their stories are not lost in this companion novel to A Stranger in Olondria.
  • With Beauty And Wonder, 'The Winged Histories' Soars

The Paper Menagerie and Other Stories

by Ken Liu

Hardcover, 450 pages

An award-winning science fiction and fantasy author hand-picks his best short-stories, as well as a brand-new piece, in a collection that includes "The Man Who Ended History: A Documentary," "Mono No Aware" and "The Waves."

  • No Paper Tiger, This 'Menagerie' Is Full Of Fierce Feeling

Borderline

by Mishell Baker

Paperback, 390 pages

Filmmaker Millicent Roper who lives with Borderline Personality Disorder and prosthetic legs after a suicide attempt becomes entangled with the mysterious Arcadia Project and finds herself working as an amateur detective while still trying to make it in Hollywood.
  • 'Borderline' Is Urban Fantasy With A Cinematic Punch

The Ballad of Black Tom

by Victor Lavalle

Paperback, 151 pages
  • 'The Ballad Of Black Tom' Offers A Tribute To And Critique Of Lovecraft

His Majesty's Dragon

by Naomi Novik

Paperback, 356 pages

When the HMS Reliant captures a French ship and its priceless cargo, an unhatched dragon egg, Captain Will Laurence is swept into an unexpected kinship with an extraordinary creature and joins the elite Aerial Corps as a master of the dragon Temaraire.
  • A Writer-Engineer's Historical Fiction Hack: Add Dragons

Uprooted

by Naomi Novik

Hardcover, 438 pages

A tale inspired by "Beauty and the Beast" follows the experiences of Agnieszka, who becomes the latest girl chosen to serve an immortal wizard who protects their village from the malevolent forces of a nearby forest.
  • A Writer-Engineer's Historical Fiction Hack: Add Dragons
  • NPR's Book Concierge: Our Guide To 2015's Great Reads
  • Friendship, Magic And Danger Blossom In 'Uprooted'

Version Control

by Dexter Palmer

Hardcover, 495 pages

A woman deals with a strange and persistent sense of everything being slightly off, which may or may not be related to her scientist husband's pet project, a "causality violation device" that might actually be working.

  • 'Version Control' Is A Dizzying Elevation Of The Time-Travel Tale

A Gathering of Shadows

by V. E. Schwab

Hardcover, 512 pages

Experiencing ominous dreams, Kell watches Red London excitedly preparing for the Element Games international magic competition only to realize that the threat of Black London is returning.

  • Plenty Of Shadows Loom In 'Gathering'

Morning Star

by Pierce Brown

Hardcover, 524 pages

Darrow emerges from years of hiding among the Golds and declares an open revolution against the overlords who oppress his people and caused the loss of his wife.
  • 'Morning Star' Brings 'Red Rising' Trilogy To An End ... Eventually

City Of Blades

by Robert Jackson Bennett

Paperback, 484 pages

After turning her back on a position most people could only dream of, General Turyin Mulaghesh, one of the most powerful people in all the Saypur empire, is sent to a backwater posting to investigate a discovery only she's qualified to make sense of, one that could change the world - or destroy it.

  • In 'City Of Blades,' The Gods Are Dead. So What Happens Next?

The Sleep Garden

by Jim Krusoe

Paperback, 341 pages

An assessment of what constitutes a life and how that life's decisions impact what remains after death is told through the intertwined stories of the living and ghostly residents of a purgatory underground apartment.

  • Strange Lives Unfold In Dreamy, Atmospheric 'Sleep Garden'

All The Birds In The Sky

by Charlie Jane Anders

Hardcover, 316 pages

Reunited as adults, childhood friends Patricia Delfine, who is magically gifted, and Laurence Armstead, an engineering genius, discover that something bigger than either of them has brought them together to either save the world, or plunge it into a new Dark Ages.

  • 'All The Birds' Overturns Sci-Fi And Fantasy, Gently

Leena Krohn: The Collected Fiction

by Leena Krohn

Hardcover, 834

Collects stories, novels and appreciations of a legendary Finnish speculative fiction author including several never-before-published English translations.
  • Finnish Authors Heat Up The Speculative Fiction World

The Deep Sea Diver's Syndrome

by Serge Brussolo

Hardcover, 196 pages

In a world where the only art form is sticky blobs of ectoplasm retrieved from lucid dreams, a professional thief makes his living stealing from jewelry stores and museums, turning loot into art for cash, until one of his dream heists goes horribly wrong.

  • A Nightmare Artist Adrift In 'Deep Sea'

Medusa's Web

by Tim Powers

Hardcover, 358 pages

Two siblings uncover a dark, supernatural family secret when they return to their childhood home in a decaying mansion in the Hollywood Hills.

  • 'Medusa's Web' Tangles The Occult And Old Hollywood

Steal the Sky

by Megan E. O'keefe

Paperback, 442 pages

When they decide to steal the airship of the exiled commodore Thratia, conman Detan Honding, who is plagued with sudden paranoia, and his trusted companion, Tibs, accidentally walk into a revolution and a crusade as Thratia prepares to wreak havoc against the Empire.

  • 'Steal The Sky' Puts A Swashbuckling Spin On Familiar Fantasy

Midnight Taxi Tango

by Daniel Jose Older

Paperback, 322 pages

An agent for the Council of the Dead who straddles the line between the living and the not-so-alive, Carlos Delacruz eliminates ghostly problems for the mortal residents of Brooklyn and investigates a string of grisly paranormal accidents in Von King Park that have put his friend Kia on edge.

  • Catch This 'Midnight Taxi' To A Magical Brooklyn
