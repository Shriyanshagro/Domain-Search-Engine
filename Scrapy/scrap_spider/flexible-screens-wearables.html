Flexible Screens To Hit The Wearable Tech This Year

By Zachary Walton
Posted on 4th April 2016
99 Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
Share
Email
Comments

Flexible screens from LG and Samsung, to hit the wearable tech market by the end of the year.

Conform to a recent article published in the ETNews, a Korean IT news site, it seems that after investing one billion in flexible OLED screens back in 2015, LG has started the mass-producing of the foldable screens. The news continues with LG’s decision to supply its foldable screens to a “corporation in the U.S. or at least with the headquarters in U.S.”

It appears that LG’s first client is a global software company. LG’s and its partner’s goal is to challenge Apple and Samsung in the high-end smartphone market with a new generation of foldable mobiles.

Interesting is that Apple already uses LG to manufacture the OLED displays for the Apple Watch. Even more interesting is the client already split the investment costs in half with LG. Also, the same client considered Samsung’s offerings first but after the negotiations decided to sign the contract with LG.

LG’s and its business partner is planning to release the next generation of products with foldable screens at some point this year. The release coincides with Samsung’s intends to introduce a foldable smartphone before the end of 2016.

Even though LG has not used the flexible screen tech in its devices, it is clear that the company has the tech, and it is ready to release it to market. For example, this January at CES, LG presented a mobile with a dual edge display, just like the Edge mobiles from Samsung.

LG’s Flexible Screens To Power The Next Generation Of Wearables?

So who’s the client? Well, only to construct the facilities needed to produce the foldable screens would cost “hundreds of millions of dollars.” Therefore, LG’s business partner is for sure a very “big fish” in the pond. Google?




Motorola also sounds like a good candidate. The company had made use of AMOLED panels in its Nexus 6 and Moto X devices. Its parent company, Lenovo, is a global player and has the money needed to invest in this new tech.

Whatever the client is, it seems that by the end of the year, the wearables and the fashion tech markets are going to benefit from the new flexible screens tech.

Flexible OLED shipment forecasts are expected to grow throughout the rest of the decade. The sales are going to increase from $3.5 billion last year to $8.2 billion by the end of 2025.
