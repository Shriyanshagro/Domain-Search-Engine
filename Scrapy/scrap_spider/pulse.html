Cut costs and gain efficiency with online expense reporting

Apr 28, 2015
  • 178 views
  • 11 Likes
  • 0 Comments
  • Share on LinkedIn
  • Share on Facebook
  • Share on Twitter

As explained last week, the innovating expense reporting system seems to enjoy less priority in many organisations. While invoicing has already been digitised in many organisations, employees are still walking around the building to print the form, get approval and bring it to the finance department. Expense management might feel as a smaller part of the job to be done at finance departments but with the right expense software it is very easy for your company to cut costs.

Right now, you’re probably thinking: ‘but software costs money. How can I save money by buying software to replace an already free process?’

Cut hidden costs

Research by the Aberdeen Group has shown that the costs of processing one expense report can be reduced with 65% with a mobile expense management application. The costs of one expense report on paper is $23. These costs are the hidden costs, based on the time employees, managers and finance spends on processing it. However, the hidden costs of an online expense report are only $5. This means a significant increase in employee convenience and productivity. First time is saved in expense report creation time for employees (27%). Next the expense approval time is reduced for managers (28%). But most time is saved at the finance department. Companies that use cloud expense management experience 50% lower expense-processing costs than companies not leveraging cloud-based expense management solutions. Because the expense reports are received digitally instead of on paper, there is no need for finance to copy the data into the accounting software. After approval, simply click on the export button. In general, expense reporting tools should integrate with every ERP. Check this with the expense software you have in mind.

By using SRXP, we have more control and managed to save a lot of costs on our expense management process.
Charles van der Puye -
Finance manager Emolife

Build your business case

Some of our largest customers have built their case whether to implement an online expense reporting tool (almost) solely on a cost-benefit analysis. In order to do that, they have not necessarily used the findings from the Aberdeen Group. Even better, they took time to analyse their own internal processing. Obviously, as they have become clients, they found that online expense reporting with SRXP in fact saves so much time that it will have a positive ROI.

Of course, there are more important benefits of online expense reporting. For example, here at SRXP we always pledge greater visibility into expenses, the frustration you will be taken away from employees and the easiness of our system. Next Tuesday, I will publish benefit no. 2: Get new insights and more control.
