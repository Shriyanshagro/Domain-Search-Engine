Case Studies

"Technology will change, but managing that change with our clients has always been our strength. We have seen many of our clients evolve from mechanical fare collection, through the era of magnetic ticketing and then finally onto smart card technology, and we have been with them every step of the way."

Steve Shewmaker, President
Cubic Transportation Systems Worldwide

 

Australasia

In 2010 Cubic, which has had a long history in providing automatic fare collection systems and services for public transport throughout the region, established a new Australasian headquarters in Sydney.

  • Case Study Brisbane
  • Case Study Sydney
 

Canada

Cubic's most recent project in Canada is the smart card and faregate system for TransLink in Vancouver which was awarded in December 2010. The contract includes the design, build and installation of the system, which will include new faregates, and will feature contactless smart card payment under TransLink's Compass brand. Following delivery, a 10-year services contract will begin.

  • Case Study Edmonton
  • Case Study Vancouver

Europe

Mainland Europe is home to one of the continent’s newest smart card ticketing and revenue management systems from Cubic.

  • Case Study Germany
  • Case Study Southern Sweden
 

United Kingdom

With a history of more than 30 years in the United Kingdom, Cubic has established itself at the forefront of enterprise management systems and services for what is considered one of the largest and most complex, and arguably the world’s finest smart card ticketing system – the Oyster® Card.

  • Case Study London

United States

Cubic has designed and delivered more transit ticketing and revenue management systems in North America than any other provider, starting with its first system for Chicago’s Illinois Central Gulf Railroad in 1972.

  • Case Study Los Angeles
  • Case Study Miami
