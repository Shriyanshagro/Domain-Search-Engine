        CLASSIC NYCB I
        • Bournonville Divertissements
        • Moves

        Two popular Balanchine ballets accompany the virtuosic Bournonville Divertissements and a Robbins work created without an orchestral score for a quintessential NYCB program.
        Running time: 2:30

        • Bournonville Divertissements

          • Music by: Edvard Helsted, Holger Simon Paulli
          • Choreography by: August Bournonville. Originally staged by Stanley Williams. Staged by Nilas Martins (2015).
          • Principal Casting: APR 22: Erica Pereira, Troy Schumacher, Sara Mearns, Tyler Angle, Sara Adams, Brittany Pollack, Indiana Woodward, Lauren King, Russell Janzen, Andrew Scordato, Anthony Huxley

            APR 23 MAT: Erica Pereira, Troy Schumacher, Abi Stafford, Zachary Catarazo, Sara Adams, Brittany Pollack, Indiana Woodward, Lauren King, Russell Janzen, Andrew Scordato, Anthony Huxley

            APR 26: Georgina Pazcoguin, Taylor Stanley, Teresa Reichlen, Ask la Cour**, Emilie Gerrity, Megan LeCrone, Meagan Mann, Ashly Isaacs (replaces Sarah Villwock), Peter Walker, Aaron Sanz, Joseph Gordon (**New York City debut)

            MAY 3: Georgina Pazcoguin, Taylor Stanley, Abi Stafford, Zachary Catarazo, Sara Adams, Megan LeCrone, Meagan Mann, Lauren King, Peter Walker, Aaron Sanz, Joseph Gordon

            MAY 5: Sara Adams, Daniel Applebaum*, Sara Mearns, Tyler Angle, Emilie Gerrity, Brittany Pollack, Indiana Woodward, Ashly Isaacs, Russell Janzen, Andrew Scordato, Anthony Huxley

          One of the few ballets in our repertory by the Danish master choreographer August Bournonville, Bournonville Divertissements provides a parade of principal roles and a cascade of nimble footwork.

        • Moves

          • Choreography by: Jerome Robbins
          • Principal Casting: APR 22, 23 MAT, 26: Emilie Gerrity*, Adrian Danchig-Waring*

            MAY 3, 5: Unity Phelan*, Taylor Stanley (*First time in role)

          Forgoing elaborate costumes, set design, and even musical accompaniment, Moves enthralls with the unexpected intensity derived from sounds produced by the dancers themselves.

        • Tschaikovsky Pas de Deux

          • Music by: Peter Ilyitch Tschaikovsky
          • Choreography by: George Balanchine
          • Principal Casting: APR 22, MAY 5: Tiler Peck, Andrew Veyette

            APR 23 MAT, MAY 3: Ashly Isaacs*, Chase Finlay (*First time in role)

            APR 26: Megan Fairchild, Gonzalo Garcia

          A virtuosic ballet, Tschaikovsky Pas de Deux is brief, beautiful, and beloved – an adrenaline rush for both dancers and audiences.

        • Symphony in Three Movements

          • Music by: Igor Stravinsky
          • Choreography by: George Balanchine
          • Principal Casting: APR 22, 23 MAT, MAY 5: Sterling Hyltin, Ana Sophia Scheller, Megan LeCrone, Taylor Stanley, Daniel Ulbricht, Joseph Gordon* (*First time in role)

            APR 26, MAY 3: Tiler Peck, Erica Pereira, Savannah Lowery, Amar Ramasar, Antonio Carmena, Andrew Scordato

          One of Balanchine’s most celebrated leotard ballets, Symphony in Three Movements is bold and breathtakingly jet-propelled, a kinetic achievement, striking for its confidence and power.

        AMERICAN MUSIC
        • Barber Violin Concerto
        • N.Y. Export: Opus Jazz
        • The Most Incredible Thing
        Buy Tickets
        One of Martins’ most popular works is joined by Robbins’ ballet in sneakers and the Winter 2016 Justin Peck premiere for a program of ballets all set to American music.
        Running time: 1:53

        • Barber Violin Concerto

          • Music by: Samuel Barber
          • Choreography by: Peter Martins
          • Principal Casting: APR 21, 7 MAT: Sara Mearns, Jared Angle, Megan Fairchild, Ask la Cour

            APR 30 EVE: Teresa Reichlen, Jared Angle, Megan Fairchild, Ask la Cour

          Alternately noble and quixotic, Barber Violin Concerto contrasts a classical couple with a bare-footed modern couple as they interchange partners in varying combinations.

        • N.Y. Export: Opus Jazz

          • Music by: Robert Prince
          • Choreography by: Jerome Robbins
          • Principal Casting: APR 21: Georgina Pazcoguin, Ashley Laracey, Justin Peck, Chase Finlay (replaces Craig Hall)

            MAY 7 MAT: Ashley Laracey, Justin Peck, Georgina Pazcoguin, Craig Hall

            APR 30 EVE: Gretchen Smith, Emilie Gerrity, Zachary Catazaro, Chase Finlay

          N.Y. Export: Opus Jazz was choreographed only a year after the landmark West Side Story opened on Broadway — the dancers in this ballet in sneakers evoke a post-war New York City with their cool jazz moves and angst-ridden beats.

        • The Most Incredible Thing

          • Music by: Bryce Dessner
          • Choreography by: Justin Peck
          • Principal Casting: APR 21, 30 EVE, MAY 7 MAT: Taylor Stanley, Sterling Hyltin, Amar Ramasar, Ask la Cour, Russell Janzen, Tiler Peck

          For his 10th ballet for NYCB, Justin Peck tackles his most ambitious work to date – The Most Incredible Thing. Featuring a cast of more than 50, Peck collaborates with composer Bryce Dessner (The National) and contemporary artist Marcel Dzama to bring Hans Christian Andersen’s dark 1870 fairy tale of the same name to the stage.

        21ST CENTURY CHOREOGRAPHERS II
        • Belles-Lettres
        • Mothership (New Bates/Blanc)
        • American Rhapsody (New Gershwin/Wheeldon)
        • Concerto DSCH
        Buy Tickets
        A recent hit and a favorite from 2008 come together with a brand new creation for the spring’s second program featuring three of today’s most sought-after choreographers.
        Running time: 1:53

        • Belles-Lettres

          • Music by: Csar Franck
          • Choreography by: Justin Peck
          • Principal Casting: MAY 7 EVE, 18: Rebecca Krohn, Lauren Lovette, Ashley Laracey, Brittany Pollack, Jared Angle, Adrian Danchig-Waring, Tyler Angle, Taylor Stanley, Anthony Huxley
            MAY 20, 21 MAT: Rebecca Krohn, Kristen Segin*, Indiana Woodward*, Brittany Pollack, Jared Angle, Adrian Danchig-Waring, Tyler Angle, Taylor Stanley, Anthony Huxley (*First time in role)

          Layering Franck's lyrical score with Mary Katrantzou's intricately embroidered lace lettered garments, Belles-Lettres is one of Peck’s most amorous ballets, replete with swooning pas de deux that build to an emotional climax.

        • Mothership (New Bates/Blanc)

          • Music by: Mason Bates
          • Choreography by: Nicolas Blanc
          • Principal Casting: MAY 4, 7 EVE, 18, 20, 21 MAT: Jackie Bologna*, Clara Frances*, Alston Macgill*+, Claire Von Enck*, Silas Farley*, Christopher Grant*+, Alec Knight*+, Sebastian Villarini-Velez* (*First time in role)(+NYCB apprentice)

          Prepare for liftoff with the four couples in Nicolas Blanc’s first work for NYCB as they launch into spinning lifts, swiveling walks, and racing slides danced to Mason Bates’ electro-acoustic orchestral score of the same name.

        • American Rhapsody (New Gershwin/Wheeldon)

          • Music by: George Gershwin
          • Choreography by: Christopher Wheeldon
          • Principal Casting: MAY 4, 7 EVE, 18, 20, 21 MAT: Tiler Peck*, Robert Fairchild*, Unity Phelan*, Amar Ramasar* (*First time in role)

          Tony Award-winner (An American in Paris) and former NYCB Resident Choreographer Christopher Wheeldon draws upon his recent Broadway success in his latest NYCB creation, set to Gershwin’s popular Rhapsody in Blue. 

        • Concerto DSCH

          • Music by: Dmitri Shostakovich
          • Choreography by: Alexei Ratmansky
          • Principal Casting: MAY 4, 7 EVE, 18: Sara Mearns, Tyler Angle, Brittany Pollack (replaces Ana Sophia Scheller), Gonzalo Garcia, Anthony Huxley
            MAY 20, 21 MAT: Sterling Hyltin*, Adrian Danchig-Waring*, Brittany Pollack, Harrison Ball, Joseph Gordon* (*First time in role)


          With its thrilling Shostakovich score and dramatic texture, Concerto DSCH excels with classical ingenuity and contemporary stylishness.

        Seminar
        Buy Tickets
        These 90-minute panel discussions will reveal how dancers, composers, and choreographers prepare for performances, from their years of training, through hours of rehearsals and finally, performances on the stage. Other collaborative artists may also speak about their designs for lighting, costumes, and scenery. Seminars are a unique opportunity to hear about the creative process and daily experiences of the artists of New York City Ballet

        Free for New York City Ballet Donors.
