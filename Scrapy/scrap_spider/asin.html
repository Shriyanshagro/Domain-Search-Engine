Answer:

In fact, that is the only way I recommend using this particular machine, because (1) the numerous complaints about bre… see more Brittany, see my 4-star lengthy, detailed review to see how I lost 165 pounds in just over two years, and how using this machine - ONLY while sitting down (never standing) - played a huge role in that. In fact, that is the only way I recommend using this particular machine, because (1) the numerous complaints about breakage and frequent repairs are a good indication that a machine of this caliber will not withstand frequent, prolonged usage in standing mode, and (2) because of the low pedal circumference, and lack of anything to balance on and/or work your upper body, you are not going to get nearly the bang for your buck, in terms of calorie burn and cardio effect, as you would with a full-on model that was only designed to be used while standing. I also believe that the low pedal circumference makes it harder on your knees if you use it while standing. With all that said, why bother standing when it will be just as effective if you use it while sitting?
My experience has shown that in order to actually see tangible weight loss, with this or any form of exercise, you have to find your personal "sweet spot" in terms of the balance of exercise and food intake (including types of foods). There's more to the big picture than that, of course, but that's a good starting point.
I would recommend setting a goal of trying to burn at least 500 calories per day with exercise - above and beyond your resting metabolism (e.g., amount of calories you would burn if you just laid in bed all day and never got up), and an additional 250-500-calorie deficit from food, but not greater than that. If you want to use this machine as a primary tool for burning calories, I recommend ignoring ALL of the readings on the LED window except the mileage counter, and set a goal of 20-30 miles each day. You can do it all at once or spaced throughout the day, but experience and research have shown me that ideally, you would break that up and space it out throughout the day, or minimally into 2 or 3 sessions. That is what works for me. I do not rely on it 100% - I also ride by bike as a form of transportation as well as for leisure - some weeks a lot, some not at all, depending on weather and other factors, and occasionally I walk to take care of errands. I also do mini HIIT and strength-training sessions once or twice a week. But by and large, after 3 years, this is still my go-to piece of equipment to ensure that I create a calorie-burning deficit most days, or at least break even. I love, love, love my portable elliptical! ~Karen :) see less
