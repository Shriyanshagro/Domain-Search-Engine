Research suggests expansion could help Big 12 in Playoff hunt.

Expansion would provide the Big 12 with an easier path to the College Football Playoff that according to number provided by a research firm hired by the league. 

Big 12 commissioner Bob Bowlsby told reporters during league meetings this week that the data presented by Navigate Research shows the conference has a slightly better chance of qualifying for the College Football Playoff semifinals - 4 to 5 percent - with the addition of two teams. The conference would also need to eliminate a conference game and add a championship game to improve its playoff odds.

It's good news for proponents of possible expansion, including leaders at UCF, USF, Connecticut, Cincinnati and Memphis.  

However the impact of adding additional team could be much larger than first reported according to Dennis Dodd of CBSSports. com. Dodd is reporting that the numbers will show that the Big 12's Playoff hopes could increase by as much as 10-15 percent.

The lower number of 4-5 percent Bowlsby quoted is reportedly based on the league only adding a championship game not expansion according to the report. 

Bowlsby said the information not been presented to school presidents, athletics directors or coaches yet.

The Big 12 currently is composed of 10 teams and plays a nine-game conference schedule. 

The benefits of expansion have been lauded by conference leaders, including Oklahoma president David Boren, who has pressed for the move during the offseason. Other officials have been less certain, asking for more information before making a decision.
