Two UCF baseball players selected in MLB Draft Wednesday
Privacy Policy

The Cincinnati Reds drafted UCF baseball players James Vasquez and Mitchell Tripp on the final day of the 2015 MLB Draft Wednesday.

Vasquez, a senior first baseman, was the No. 745th pick in the 25th round while Tripp, a redshirt junior pitcher, got his name called a short time later as the No. 1,075 pick in the 36th round. The Knights saw shortstop Dylan Moore picked up by the Texas Rangers Tuesday as the No. 198th pick in the 10th round.

Currently, the Reds have picked five UCF baseball players to date in the draft including Ben Lively (4th round, 2013), Rich Lawrence (11th round, 1995 and Chad Mottola (1st round, 1992).

Moore's selection by the Rangers made him the first player to be drafted by the franchise from UCF.

With the selections of Vasquez and Tripp, coach Terry Rooney has now sent 21 players to the MLB Draft since taking over the program in 2008.
