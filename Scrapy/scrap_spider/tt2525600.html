A Christmas special...just like the ones I used to know.
13 December 2012 | by Carycomic (Torrington, CT, USA) – See all my reviews

My mother and I caught this on TV the other night. And, it was awesome! I grew up watching the annual Holiday specials telecast by Bing Crosby, Bob Hope, and Perry Como. I even remember a Xmas special hosted by Mac Davis that featured guest Robert Urich singing a carol in a European dialect called Old Church Slavonic!

And, now, Mr. Buble' has revived that venerable tradition. For which, I profusely thank him. Not only for his imaginative and computer-enhanced duet, with Bing, on the Irving Berlin classic "White Christmas." But, also, for the unintended chuckle during the duet, with Rod Stewart, on "Winter Wonderland."

Or, didn't anyone else realize it was two _men_ singing about Parson Brown asking if they were married? Lol!
