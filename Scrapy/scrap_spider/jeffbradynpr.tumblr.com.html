Americans used less energy in 2015

The latest US energy flow chart is out from Lawrence Livermore National Lab. Here’s an interesting take-away from the press release:

“Renewable energy continues to grow, with use of wind energy up 5 percent, geothermal energy up 11 percent and residential solar energy up 11 percent. Utility scale solar energy, which saw the completion of several major projects in recent years, was up 25 percent.”

Happy 100th Birthday Beverly Cleary! 

My favorite Ramona scene (from Beezus and Ramona):

Ramona was sitting on the floor beside a box of apples. Lying around her on the cement floor were a number of apples – each with one bite out of it. While Beezus stared, Ramona reached into the box, selected an apple, took one big bite out of the reddest part, and tossed the rest of the apple onto the floor. While she noisily chewed that bite, she reached into the apple box again.

“Ramona!” cried Beezus, horrified. “You can’t do that.”

“I can, too,” said Ramona through her mouthful.

“Stop it,” ordered Beezus. “Stop it this instant! You can’t eat one bite and then throw the rest away.”

“But the first bite tastes best,” explained Ramona reasonably, as she reached into the box again.


Read the NPR tribute to Beverly Cleary. Oregon Public Broadcasting has a page dedicated to her too.

Solar And Wind Energy May Be Nice, But How Can We Store It?

One problem with solar power is that the sun goes down. A lot of people are working to store energy from the sun during the day to use at night.

I toured a Nevada plant that stores the sun’s heat in molten salt, which then powers a steam generator after sundown.

My colleagues Lauren Sommer, Leigh Patterson examined two other storage options that are not batteries: air and ice. Read more or listen to our NPR pieces here.
