Mott MacDonald confirmed as design partner for Balfour Beatty / Vinci HS2 team

25 April, 2016 By Gavriel Hollander

  • Comment

The Balfour Beatty / Vinci joint venture bidding for some of the £11.8bn High Speed 2 civils packages has named Mott MacDonald and French firm Systra as its design partners.

The JV was last month shortlisted for four of the five most valuable works packages available on HS2, with a combined total value of up to £5.4bn.

Consortiums bidding for HS2 work are putting together their design teams, with partners now in place for most bidders.

Last week, Construction News revealed that Jacobs and Ingerop-Rendel had been appointed design partners for the Align JV, featuring Bouygues, Sir Robert McAlpine and VolkerFitzpatrick.

In March, WSP Parsons Brinckerhoff was named as partner for the Laing O’Rourke / Murphy / FCC Construcción JV.

Other design partners confirmed for HS2 bidding teams include Aecom, working with the Morgan Sindall / Bam Nuttall / Ferrovial ‘Fusion’ JV; Arup, which is on board with the Costain / Skanska / Strabag consortium; and Ineco, which is partnering with Dragados / Galliford Try / Hochtief.

Read more on HS2 

See the full HS2 phase one civils shortlist here

How do the £11.8bn civils joint ventures compare?

Mott MacDonald has been involved with HS2 on the client side for the past six years. The consultant provided engineering and environmental services for the London section of the project, and was also appointed last year to work on route proposals for phase two of the line, north of Birmingham.

Systra says it has been involved in every high-speed rail link developed in its native France and 50 per cent of those worldwide. In the UK it has worked on rail projects including Crossrail and the Edinburgh-Glasgow line improvement programme.

Preferred bidders for the seven civils packages on phase one are expected to be announced next year.
