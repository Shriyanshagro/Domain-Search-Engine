Ex-Umphrey's McGee drummer dies Tribune Staff Report SouthBendTribune.com

Mike Mirro, an original member of the rock band Umphreys McGee, formerly based in South Bend, has died at age 36, according to several online sites.

Mirro was the first drummer in the band, which was formed at the University of Notre Dame in 1997 and has built a strong fan base worldwide.

He departed the band in 2002 to attend medical school.

No cause of death has been reported at this time.

The bands website posted this message on Thursday:

It is with heavy hearts that we have learned of our good friend & former drummer Mike Mirros passing. As many of you know, Mikes love for music and unique sense of humor are still present today in the music that we create. Umphreys McGee wouldnt be who we are without all of the passion & musicality Mike added to the music for the first 5 years of our career.

There are no words that can convey the feeling of loss we are collectively feeling, we have lost a friend, a brother, and a man without whom there would be no Umphreys McGee. Mikey, we will always love you for the amazing person that you were and well carry your spirit with us through the music.

Well miss you.

The music website Relix reported that Mirros final project with the band came in the form of Local Band Does OK, which coincidentally was the first to feature guitarist Jake Cinninger and percussionist Andy Farag. Following the 2002 tour, Mirro announced his departure from the band to attend medical school. Umphreys then called on Kick the Cat drummer Kris Myers, who currently serves as drummer.

Relix also reported that in 2011 Mirro joined the band during its annual UMBowl event in Chicago to reprise several performances.

According to the website Memorial Networks, visitation will be from 2 to 8 p.m. CST Tuesday at Pruzin & Little Funeral Service, 811 E. Franciscan Drive, Crown Point. A funeral Mass will be said at 10 a.m. CST Wednesday at St. Mary Catholic Church, 321 Joliet St., Crown Point. Services to conclude at the church with cremation to follow. In lieu of flowers, memorials are preferred to the Michael A. Mirro Fund for Neuroscience Research at the Parkview Foundation.
