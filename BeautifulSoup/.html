Information leaks help US traders turn a fat profit

Description
Research into trading in US stock and bond markets by Royal Holloway economist Alessio Sancetta suggests that some traders might be making a significant profit from information leaks.
Date:

Delayed onset adulthood keeps young Brits away from ballot box

Description
﻿The poor voter turnout of young Brits can be explained by the delayed transition to adulthood, says new research from Royal Holloway politics expert Kaat Smets. And as early voting behaviour trends set the pattern for life-long voting, Dr Smets says it is not likely that current low levels of participation will see an improvement.
Date:
05/05/2016

Professor of German's lifetime achievements recognised by major award

Description
Professor of German W. Daniel Wilson has been awarded the Reimar Lüst Award for International Scholarly and Cultural Exchange, in recognition of his achievements in research and teaching. The prestigious award carries a prize of 60,000 Euros.

Date
13/06/2016
Location:
Windsor Building Auditorium
Description
In Terrorism and Tolerance, David Anderson will ask to what extent international law can help us to reconcile free society with the prevention of extremism. Under real threat of terrorism, both chronic and acute, the UK's multi-pronged response has been influential and in many respects successful. But a controversial element of that response has been the Prevent strategy, recently extended beyond the field of terrorism by the Government's counter-extremism strategy of October 2015.
